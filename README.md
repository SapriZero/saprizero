# A New Treatise on Zero

*A formal reconstruction of zero as tension between opposites, deriving the golden ratio and six irreducible generators.*

---

## Why "Sapri Zero"?

The name *Sapri* is not accidental. It is a small coastal town in southern Italy, known for its rugged beauty, the calm of its landscapes, and its historical significance (the landing of Carlo Pisacane). But there is another reason.

In Italian, *sapri* is a dialectal contraction of *si apri* — "it opened". This work is precisely about that: **zero opening itself**, revealing the tension between two opposite poles, and from that tension generating numbers, geometry, and the constants that shape our universe.

The name also reflects the spirit of the place where these ideas took form — a place of quiet, of nature, of distance from the frenzy of academic noise. In that silence, the logic of zero could speak clearly.

---

## Overview

This work presents a rigorous logical reconstruction of the concept of zero, not as a numerical value, but as the state of tension between two opposite indeterminate instances: $|-|$ — zero seen from inside.

From this primitive, we derive:
- the natural numbers as logical roles (distance, stability, plane, volume, rhythm, network, mass);
- the arithmetic operations;
- the golden ratio $\varphi$ as the unique residue of a closed cycle;
- six irreducible generators: the geometric triad $\pi$, $\varphi$, $\sqrt{2}$ and the physical triad $\alpha$, $\mu$, $h$, which form the minimal set from which all dimensionless constants of physics can be derived.

The **Sapri Aurea formula** emerges as a direct consequence, providing a closed‑form approximation for the complete elliptic integral of the first kind using the golden ratio as exponent.

The construction is made fully explicit and executable using the **lambda calculus**, showing that the entire framework is not a philosophical narrative but a computable structure grounded in the same logic (AND, OR, NOT) that underlies digital computation.
